Election Timeline
A complete walkthrough of the election calendar from start to finish.
📢
Phase 1 · ~18 months before
Announcement & Filing
Candidates publicly announce their intention to run and file official paperwork with the relevant election authority. This includes submitting signatures, paying filing fees, and disclosing financials.
Candidate DeclarationFEC FilingNomination Petitions
🏟️
Phase 2 · ~12 months before
Primary Elections & Caucuses
Political parties hold internal contests to choose their official nominee. Voters within each party select their preferred candidate through primaries (direct voting) or caucuses (group discussion meetings).
Open PrimariesClosed PrimariesCaucusesDelegates
🎉
Phase 3 · ~6 months before
Party Conventions
Major parties hold national conventions where delegates officially nominate their presidential and vice-presidential candidates. Party platforms are finalized and candidates deliver their acceptance speeches.
DelegatesParty PlatformVice President Pick
📣
Phase 4 · 3–6 months before
General Election Campaign
Candidates campaign nationwide, hold debates, run advertisements, and canvas voters. Campaign finance laws regulate spending and disclosure. Voter registration deadlines fall within this period.
DebatesVoter Registration DeadlineCampaign AdsGround Game
🗳️
Phase 5 · Election Day
Voting Day
Registered voters cast their ballots at polling stations or via mail-in/absentee ballots. Polls typically open from 6 AM to 8 PM local time. Poll workers and independent observers oversee the process.
Polling StationsMail-in BallotsExit PollsPoll Workers
📊
Phase 6 · Days to Weeks After
Vote Counting & Certification
Ballots are counted and results are tallied at the county and state levels. Election officials certify the results after verifying all counts, resolving provisional ballots, and conducting any required recounts.
CanvassingRecountsCertificationElectoral College Vote
How to Vote
Your step-by-step guide to participating in an election.
01
Check Your Eligibility
Confirm you meet requirements: citizenship, age (18+), residency in your jurisdiction, and no disqualifying legal status.
02
Register to Vote
Complete voter registration before the deadline. You can register online, by mail, or in person at a government office.
03
Research Candidates & Issues
Review candidates' platforms, ballot measures, and local issues. Check your sample ballot in advance so you're prepared.
04
Find Your Polling Place
Look up your assigned polling location and hours. Know what ID you need to bring based on your state's requirements.
05
Cast Your Ballot
Check in with poll workers, receive your ballot, mark your choices privately, and submit it. Your vote is anonymous.
06
Confirm Your Vote Counted
Many jurisdictions offer vote tracking for mail-in ballots. You can also contact your local election office to verify.
